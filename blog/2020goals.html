2019 was an incredible milestone year that paved the way for my increased success, happiness, and maturity. I went through many life changes including graduating college and entering the workforce. I am very excited to find my groove in 2020 and continue to grow personally and professionally.

The usefullness does not stop at coding, however. It's a nifty device to have for a multitude of reasons. Wherever you might need a computer (albeit a modest one), the raspberry pi is a perfect little device that has all the necessary components in a regular computer. I originally purchased a Raspberry Pi (model 4) to keep a music server running on my network at home, but quickly realized the world of opportunities this cheap little computer opens up. These are the top softwares that I think are necessary for any hobbyist to keep on their devices as well.

Raspotify - A Spotify Music Server

This program was the reason I bought my device. It serves a small purpose, and you would never know it was there, but it makes my life marginally easier.

With Raspotify running on my device, I always have an option to use my Raspberry Pi as an audio output for Spotify, wherever I am. It's currently hooked up to my surround system in my living room (which has no bluetooth or internet capabilities).

[github]

Pi-Hole Network-Wide Ad Blocking

Pi-Hole runs on a raspberry pi device as an interface between a modem and router, so that you can blacklist common websites used by advertisers for an ad-free experience on an entire network.

You can also view special network statistics about how many ads have been blocked--the numbers are staggering!

[github]

Plex Media Server

Plex Media Server (sometimes called PMS or PMS Software) is the back-end media server component of Plex. It organizes audio and visual content from personal media libraries and streams it to their player counterparts, either on the same machine, the same local area network, or over the Internet.

Plex does an ace job at hosting and streaming your content also organising media to include metadata such as trailers, ratings and reviews and more. Another bonus is you'll find a Plex app on almost any device such as Android, iOS, Xbox/PS and Smart TVs as well access via the Web UI.

[github]

Minecraft Server

I sometimes play Minecraft but felt it was necessary to run my own server on my own terms so I can invite friends and family as well! I don't use it often, but nonetheless it was a fun project to complete.

In conclusion, I believe every tech hobbyist should invest in a Raspberry Pi. It's very cheap (less than $50), and can entertain a curious mind for days on end. There are projects out there for every person, and may inspire one to create a project of their own.Thanks for reading!
